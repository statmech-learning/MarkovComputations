# Post-Phase-3 Markov-ICL Synthesis

## Executive Answer

`gamma*_ICL` is repaired on the analytic toy gate. The previous failure was localized mainly to the Toy B branch-data definition: the one-branch max task must contain both `M1>` and `M2>`, not a single active branch. With delta-separated branch data and the paper's analytic no-bias constructions, Toy A fails as expected, Toy B passes, and Toy C passes.

The input-multiplicity causal control gives supportive but not final causal evidence. In the existing fixed-m20 trained mask library, normalized tree-difference comparison overlap improves grouped LOO prediction beyond physical-graph/count/`d_rel` controls, while edge-level multiplicity does not. The matched high-low contrast is positive but its bootstrap interval crosses zero, so this should guide the next prospective exact-control run rather than be treated as a universal scalar law.

## Required Questions

1. Was `gamma*_ICL` repaired on analytic toy cases?

   Yes. `gamma_toy_repair_final_report.md` reports `Gamma repaired: True`.

2. If not, which diagnostic failed?

   Not applicable after repair. The earlier failure was localized to branch-data and probe-definition issues: Toy B was encoded as one active branch/class instead of the max-branch pair `M1>,M2>`. Tree orientation now matches the paper's `A_i,B_i,C_i` tree-sum list, and analytic warm starts are preserved or improved by optimization.

3. Does tree-level or tree-difference multiplicity causally predict ICL under matched controls?

   Existing-data answer: tree-difference multiplicity is the stronger candidate. For mean novel-class ICL, grouped LOO `R2` is `0.376` for controls, `0.226` for edge-level features plus controls, `0.388` for tree-level features plus controls, and `0.452` for tree-difference features plus controls. The strict `d_rel=200` subset gives `0.293` for tree-difference plus controls versus `0.187` for controls.

4. Does the causal result support Phase 2 or show confounding?

   It supports Phase 2 directionally, but with caveats. The fixed-m20 library was not prospectively generated by exact tree-overlap matching, and the matched high-low tree-difference contrast is `+2.195` mean novel-ICL points with a 95% bootstrap interval of `[-0.882, 5.122]`. That is design evidence, not a completed causal proof.

5. Which metric should be used for the next exact-degree normal-fan expansion?

   Use normalized same-root tree-difference comparison overlap as the primary mask metric, with normalized tree-level comparison overlap and coordinate/load Gini as stratifiers. Edge-level multiplicity should be retained only as a baseline. Repaired no-bias `gamma*_ICL` can now be computed as a diagnostic predictor, but it should still be evaluated under exact-control held-out checks before being used as a broad selector.

6. Which claims are supported, weakened, and open?

   Supported: first-order CRN-ICL should measure input multiplicity in rooted-tree and tree-difference space; the analytic small-system gate for `gamma*_ICL` now passes; tree-difference overlap is the best current candidate for a branch-aware pretraining mask functional.

   Weakened: edge-level multiplicity as a sufficient input-mask summary; the earlier failed gamma report as evidence against branch-margin theory.

   Open: a prospective exact-degree, exact-`d_rel`, exact-multiplicity causal training experiment; branch-failure and trained-margin validation for fixed-m20 masks; thermodynamic force-budget claims.

## Expressivity

The repaired toy results restore consistency with the first-order tree-sum theory. Two species cannot cover both max and min branch directions, two species can solve the max-branch pair, and the complete three-species directed graph can cover both branches through the paper's tree-sum construction.

## Trainability

The analytic warm starts are not merely classified correctly; L-BFGS preserves or improves their lower-tail margin. This makes `gamma*_ICL` usable again as a candidate capacity diagnostic. It is not yet a trainability law for large topology families.

## Mechanism

The mechanism evidence remains branch/projection/tree alignment. The new input-mask result sharpens this: the mask matters through how comparison coordinates co-participate in rooted trees and same-root tree differences, not through isolated edge counts alone.

## Causal Evidence

The fixed-m20 existing-data control holds input-coupled parameter count and aggregate `M_mean` fixed, uses physical graph controls, and summarizes seeds within mask/topology groups. Tree-difference features improve grouped LOO prediction and produce a positive matched high-low contrast. Because exact input-edge-count matching is incomplete and the confidence interval crosses zero, the result is supportive but not final.

## Thermodynamic Physics

No thermodynamic `Fmax` experiment was run. No thermodynamic claim is supported yet. That phase still requires reversible support, detailed-balance verification at `Fmax=0`, stable generator conventions, and a valid comparison to the first-order CRN-ICL task.
